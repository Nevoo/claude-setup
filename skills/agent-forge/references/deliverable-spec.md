# The roadmap deliverable — client-facing report spec

The deliverable is what the client pays for AND the thing that sells the build. A non-technical owner must get it on the first read, and it must lead with money. This is the sales layer; the Agent Blueprint is the build layer behind it.

## Structure (in order)

1. **Executive summary** — 3–4 sentences. The dollar ROI number sits in the first line.
2. **ROI, computed from their numbers** — hours saved per week × their stated loaded hourly cost = $/month and $/year. Show the arithmetic. If the rate wasn't captured, write the formula with `[rate to confirm]` rather than inventing one. No made-up savings, ever; every figure traces to something they said.
3. **In your words** — 2–3 short verbatim quotes from the call naming the pain, in their language. Not paraphrased. This is what makes it sound like their business.
4. **The three biggest time sinks** — plain English, no jargon.
5. **Impact vs. effort** — each candidate workflow placed by business impact against build effort. Quick wins (high impact, low effort) called out first.
6. **Recommended first build** — the strongest candidate as an Agent Blueprint summary: what it does, what it saves, the 30-day good-enough. Recommendations point to **agents arlou can build**, not a generic tool directory.
7. **What not to automate yet** — and why. Builds trust and pre-empts over-reach.
8. **Next step** — the build, scoped.

## Rules

- Money first, jargon never.
- Quote them; don't translate their pain into your words.
- Every number is traceable to the transcript. Mark unknowns as unknown.
- Recommend builds you can actually deliver.
- The acceptance check for this deliverable: the owner understands it without you in the room, and the ROI number is the first thing they see.

## Optional summary lens

If it clarifies a given client, score their current state on three axes: **Effectiveness** (doing the right work), **Efficiency** (doing it fast), **Quality** (output is good). Use only when it adds clarity. The workflow map and the ROI are the substance; this is packaging.
